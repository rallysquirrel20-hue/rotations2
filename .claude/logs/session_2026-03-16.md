## Fix: `get_live_ohlc_bars()` — impossible candlestick bars (Close > High)

### What changed
Removed "Part B" of `get_live_ohlc_bars()` (~55 lines) which overwrote the Close price from 1-minute bar aggregation with a bid/ask mid-price from Databento's live `mbp-1` feed. This mid-price could land outside the aggregated High/Low range, producing impossible candlestick bars where Close exceeded High. Also removed the now-unused inner helpers (`_decode`, `_scale`, `_UNDEF_PRICE`) and updated the docstring.

### Root cause
Live tickers (e.g. RVMD, EFX, CHTR, CBRE) displayed Close prices significantly higher than High. The `mbp-1` mid-price used in Part B was not constrained to the OHLC range derived from 1-minute bar aggregation in Part A.

### Why Part A alone is correct
Part A aggregates 1-minute bars and uses `grp['close'].iloc[-1]` as the session Close. This value is the last traded price from actual 1-minute bars, guaranteed to fall within the High/Low range by construction (since High/Low are the max/min of those same bars).

### Files modified
1. **rotations_signals/rotations.py** — `get_live_ohlc_bars` (was lines 4201–4320, now ~4201–4264): removed Part B live feed subscription, mid-price calculation, close override, and dead helper functions.

### Status
Complete — user confirmed "that did the trick."
